The Art of Color

Color is one of the most powerful tools in interior design. It can influence mood, alter the perception of space, and tie a whole room together. Our guide will help you understand the basics of color theory and apply it with confidence. A great place to start is the 60-30-10 Rule: 60% of your room should be a dominant color, 30% a secondary color, and 10% an accent color.

Earthy Neutrals

This calming, nature-inspired palette is perfect for creating a serene and grounded space. It pairs well with natural wood, linen textiles, and lots of plants. Ideal for living rooms and bedrooms.

Coastal Blues

Bring the breezy feel of the coast indoors. This palette uses soft blues and sandy beiges to create a light, airy, and relaxing atmosphere. It's perfect for bathrooms, bedrooms, and sunrooms.

Warm & Vibrant

For a space that feels energetic and inviting, this palette combines warm terracotta with soft blush tones. It's great for creative spaces, dining areas, or anywhere you want to encourage conversation.

Moody & Modern

Create a sophisticated and dramatic look with this high-contrast palette. Deep charcoal or navy provides a bold backdrop for warm leather and metallic accents. Excellent for home offices or accent walls.
